01

The logo, and how it breathes

Cream is the home colour. Always give the illustration room.
Primary · cream
Reverse · sage
Deep · ocean

Clear space

Keep at least the height of "C" in COASTAL clear on every side. The illustration's wave + leaves are part of the mark — never crop them.

Minimum size

Full lockup: 56px tall on screen, 22mm in print. Below that, use the wave-and-leaf bug alone with a separate wordmark.

Don't stretch or recolour the linework

The illustration is hand-drawn — it should always sit on cream, sage, or deep ocean. Avoid placing on photographs without a tinted plate behind it.

Don't add a tagline inside the wreath

If a tagline is needed, set it underneath in the serif italic. The wreath stays as the mark drew it.

60 / 30 / 10

60% home base (cream/bone) · 30% sage family · 10% deep accent (ocean/eucalypt) plus sparse use of the warm accent (driftwood/banksia) for sale, urgency, or seasonal callouts.

One palette per surface

Don't mix Foreshore and Hinterland in the same view. They're sister directions; pick one for retail, one for trade if needed — never both at once.

Sage stays sage

The logo sage (#8EA08A) is the shared anchor. Cream, seafoam, driftwood and ocean extend it. Ochre and Banksia are accent-only — never backgrounds.

Accent layer — use minimally

Ochre & Banksia

Two warm tones from the native heathland palette. Use at under 5% of any surface — exclusively for sale flags, urgency cues, stock alerts and seasonal highlights. Never as a body background or primary button fill.

Rule of thumb

If you're unsure whether to use it — don't. One accent element per page is usually enough.
