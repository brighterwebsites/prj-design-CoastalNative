About this plant

A tough coastal all-rounder

The Coast Banksia is one of the most reliable trees for exposed coastal sites. Salt-tolerant, wind-hardy, and fast-establishing, it provides year-round food for honeyeaters and provides natural windbreak character from the first season. The golden cylindrical flower spikes appear April through July, making it one of the most rewarding large trees for wildlife gardens.

Provenance-collected seed from the Bellarine Peninsula coastal heathland community. Stock grown at our Marshall nursery and hardened to coastal conditions before lifting.
